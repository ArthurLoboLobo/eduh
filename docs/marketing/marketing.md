# Marketing

Strategy for getting Eduh in front of real users. Initial audience: STEM
students (engineering + CS) at Unicamp and USP, reached through Arthur's
personal network.

## Positioning

**Sell "a better way to actually study."** The wedge is pedagogical
structure. Studying with a raw LLM chat means you have to know what to ask,
there's no sequence, no progress, no one checking if you understood. Eduh
replaces the blank chat box with a real study flow:

- **A plan, not a chat.** Reads your materials and produces an ordered list of topics and subtopics to study.
- **Topic-by-topic teaching.** Each topic follows a real sequence: concept → deeper → check understanding → worked example → problem to solve → feedback → next topic.
- **Progress you can see.** Topics marked complete, progress bar, timeline.
- **Tied to a specific test, using your materials.** Plan targets one exam and uses your slides, notes, and past tests via RAG. The teaching is grounded in what you'll actually be tested on.
- **STEM-native rendering.** LaTeX and code blocks render properly.

**Do not sell "AI tutor."** ChatGPT/Gemini/NotebookLM already own that phrase in students' heads.

### 10-second pitch (pt-BR)

> "Sabe quando você tenta estudar com ChatGPT e fica meio perdido? O Eduh lê
> os PDFs da sua matéria, monta um plano e te ensina tópico por tópico —
> explica o conceito, faz exemplo resolvido, te dá exercício e confere se
> entendeu. Se você é da Unicamp ou USP, ganha 1 mês grátis."

### Order of the pitch

1. Lead: "estuda melhor do que num chat solto" (plan + pedagogical flow).
2. Follow: uses your real materials, built around a specific test.
3. Close: free month for Unicamp/USP.

## Audience

**Primary: Unicamp and USP STEM students**, especially engineering and CS.
- High-stakes tests with clear dates — timing hooks work.
- Strong past-test culture.
- LaTeX/math/code-heavy materials — plays to our rendering.
- Arthur can reach them warmly (2nd-year CS at Unicamp).

**Secondary: USP**, reached only via 1–2 bridge friends. No cold posting.

## The Bonus Hook

Unicamp/USP emails unlock **R$20 in credits = 1 month Pro free** (see `docs/subscription/subscription.md` → Bonus). Strongest conversion lever we have for this audience. Frame as "já que você é da Unicamp" — a perk of their identity, not a discount. Mention it last.

## Distribution (in order of expected ROI)

1. **Time it to P2s and finais.** April–May is P2 season at Unicamp. "Tô usando pra estudar pra P2 de MC202, quer testar?" converts dramatically better than a cold "check out my app." Every move below should be timed to a test in the recipient's next 1–2 weeks.
2. **1:1 DMs before group posts.** First ~15 users come from personalized messages to close friends, ideally with a screenshot of *their* course already loaded in. These become the social proof for wider posts.
3. **Course-specific WhatsApp groups.** "Turma MC322 2026" style groups — everyone has the same test. Post a short demo using *that* course's materials. Avoid giant general groups for the first wave.
4. **60-second demo video.** One reusable asset, built around a feared disciplina (Cálculo III, MC404, Física III). Upload → plan → chat answering a real past-test question with LaTeX. Reuse in DMs, groups, stories.
5. **Reps de turma and CAs.** One rep sharing = ~40 students. Warm asks only. CACo/CAEC come later, after organic traction.
6. **USP via bridge friends.** Never post directly. Bridge friends say "meu amigo da Unicamp fez isso" with the free-month hook.

## What to Avoid

- Comparing directly to ChatGPT in copy — invites the reader to imagine using ChatGPT instead.

## Metrics (informal, for now)

- Unicamp/USP signups in the first 2 weeks.
- Promo claims (signals engagement with the paywall).
- Sections that reach the Studying phase (real activation).
- Most common disciplinas in section names (where demand is).
